How I Read a Complicated Canva Resume
🔹 1. Ignore the Design Initially – Focus on Content
I mentally strip away colors, shapes, icons, and background patterns.

I look for key sections: Name, Title, Contact, Summary, Experience, Skills, Education.

If things are scattered visually, I find the reading order based on logical flow, not visuals.

Think of it like reading a magazine layout—scan top-left to bottom-right, identifying the story.

🔹 2. Identify Redundancies & Distractions
Design-heavy resumes often include:

Duplicated sections (as in your resume)

Irrelevant graphs (e.g., “Teamwork 94%” – subjective)

Icons that don’t communicate much (e.g., speech bubble ≠ communication)

Overloaded sidebars with fluff

I evaluate: “Does this section help me understand the candidate’s value? If not, remove or reword it.”

🔹 3. Manually Reconstruct Linear Flow
Since Canva resumes are often not in a traditional top-down layout, I mentally (or literally) reconstruct the flow into a standard order:

Header & Contact

Summary / Profile

Skills

Work Experience

Projects (if any)

Education

Extras

This helps me:

Compare it to job descriptions

Scan for keywords

Assess storytelling

🔹 4. Check for ATS Compatibility
Many Canva resumes are not ATS-friendly because:

Text may be in text boxes (ATS systems can’t read them)

Icons, lines, and graphics may confuse scanners

Some use tables or images for text

So I copy-paste sections into a blank document to test if it’s real readable text.

🔹 5. Focus on Quantifiable & Action-Oriented Content
Even in a complex layout, I ask:

Are job roles described with impact or just duties?

Are there numbers (e.g., “Trained 10 team members”, “Reduced errors by 25%”)?

Are verbs powerful? (e.g., "Led", "Improved", "Implemented")

If not, I rewrite or guide candidates to do so.

🛠 Pro Tip: How You Can Do It Too
If you're evaluating your own or someone else's Canva CV:

✅ What to Do
Read top-down, left-right, ignoring the shapes

Extract key sections and paste into a blank doc to see what’s “real” text

Translate visuals (graphs, icons) into readable data

Reorder content in your head (or on paper) into a standard resume structure

Check for consistency in fonts, colors, and spacing

🚫 What to Avoid
Relying on pretty visuals to carry weak content

Using non-standard fonts or unreadable layouts

Placing critical content in sidebars or narrow boxes

Including non-actionable or vague information (like “94% Communication”)
