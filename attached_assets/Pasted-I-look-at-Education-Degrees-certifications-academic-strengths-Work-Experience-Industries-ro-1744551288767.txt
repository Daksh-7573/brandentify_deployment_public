I look at:

Education: Degrees, certifications, academic strengths.

Work Experience: Industries, roles, achievements, duration, progression.

Skills: Technical, soft, languages, tools.

Projects: Independent or collaborative, relevance to career goals.

Interests & Hobbies: Often overlooked, but they hint at passion-based careers.

Volunteering & Extra-curriculars: Leadership, teamwork, social responsibility.

🔍 2. Identify Core Strengths and Transferable Skills
I highlight:

What they do best (e.g., problem solving, leadership, writing, tech skills).

Skills that are valuable across industries (e.g., project management, communication, data analysis).

🎯 3. Understand Their Personality, Goals & Preferences
If they’re open to it, I’ll ask about:

Career goals (money, passion, impact, balance?).

Work style (remote, flexible, structured, high-pressure?).

Values (stability, growth, creativity, social good?).

Long-term vision (corporate ladder, freelancing, entrepreneurship?).

🔄 4. Suggest Tailored Career Paths
Based on the CV and preferences, I propose:

Traditional roles they are already suited for.

Adjacent roles where their skills are transferable.

Emerging career paths they might not know of yet.

Creative or unconventional options if their background supports it.

Example:

If someone has experience in marketing and loves writing, I might suggest roles in content strategy, UX writing, brand consulting, or even building a personal brand as a creator.

📚 5. Recommend Upskilling Paths (if needed)
I’ll suggest:

Courses, certifications, or tools to master.

Skills in demand in their target industries.

Ways to build a portfolio or gain real-world experience (e.g., freelancing, internships).

🧭 6. Create a Career Roadmap
This includes:

Short-term goals (next role, skill-building).

Medium-term goals (domain expertise, leadership, relocation).

Long-term vision (entrepreneurship, management, legacy work).

🧠 Bonus: Mindset and Strategy
Tips on how to network effectively.

How to tailor resumes for each role.

Interview prep ideas.

Encouragement to explore, pivot, and experiment.
