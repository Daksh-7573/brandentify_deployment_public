The Big Picture

Earlier, Brandentifier’s Brand Quests were smart but generic — everyone got daily tasks like “Share an insight” or “Update your profile”.
Now, the system will understand who you are, what you want to achieve, and where you’re trying to grow — and then create personalized daily quests that make sense for you specifically.

It’s like turning your professional growth into a smart, gamified assistant that knows your goals.

🎯 What’s New & Improved
1️⃣ You Choose Your Focus (Brand Goals)

You now tell the system what matters most to you by choosing up to 3 Brand Goals (from 12 total).

For example:

You might pick:
✅ Increase visibility on social media
✅ Attract new business opportunities
✅ Build a loyal community

This instantly gives the system direction — “Okay, this person wants visibility, leads, and engagement.”
So, your quests now revolve around these areas — not random content.

2️⃣ Your Profile Adds Personality (New Fields)

Your profile data is no longer just static information — it becomes the “brain” of your Brand Quest personalization.

The system now looks at:

Field	What it’s used for
Job Title & Industry	Decides what kind of professional you are (e.g. Designer, Marketer, Developer).
Domain (sub-industry)	Helps tailor content ideas — e.g., “FinTech”, “Healthcare”, “Ecommerce”.
Location	Suggests relevant hashtags, connection opportunities, or time to post.
About Me & Mission	Helps craft personalized tones and story-based quests.
What I Offer	Generates quests to promote your services/products naturally.
Looking For	Adjusts networking quests — e.g., “connect with mentors”, “engage with investors”.
Core Values & Vision Statement	Adds emotional and brand-aligned angles to posts.
Primary & Secondary Audience	Helps system pick right tone and topics — B2B, D2C, Founders, etc.
Unique Value Proposition	Drives “Authority” and “Career Growth” style quests.

So now, your Brandentifier profile acts like your brand DNA — every quest generated knows your industry, goals, and voice.

⚙️ How Quest Generation Works (Simple Flow)

Let’s simplify the logic behind the improved system 👇

🧩 Step 1: System Understands You

When you log in (or at midnight auto-scheduler runs):

It scans your saved profile fields.

It checks your selected Brand Goals (max 3).

It builds your “Brand Personality Card” — a short internal summary like:

“SaaS Marketer from India, targeting business owners in the US. Focused on Visibility, Community, and Monetization.”

⚙️ Step 2: System Chooses What Type of Quests to Give

The system decides what kind of quests to assign for the day:

Career Quests (on-platform): improve your profile, add projects, post to Industry Pulse, make connections.

Social Quests (off-platform): LinkedIn, Instagram, X, YouTube, etc. — content and networking actions.

It then balances them (1–2 per day) based on your chosen goals.

Example:

If your goal = Visibility → more posting and Pulse quests.

If your goal = Career Growth → more case study, portfolio, and authority-building quests.

If your goal = Engagement → more polls, comments, and connection quests.

If your goal = Monetization → more lead-generation and soft-selling quests.

🧠 Step 3: Personalization Kicks In

Once the quest type is selected, the system personalizes the text using your profile data.

Example template before personalization:

“Share your {{uniqueExpertise}} with {{targetAudience}} to show your impact.”

After personalization (based on your data):

“Share your Ecommerce marketing expertise with D2C brand founders in Mumbai to show how your campaigns boost conversions.”

So now the same quest feels like it’s written just for you.

🧩 Step 4: Platform Intelligence

Next, the system chooses which platform is best for that task:

A designer might get an Instagram post quest.

A writer might get a LinkedIn article quest.

A startup founder might get a Twitter thread or Brandentifier Pulse post.

It uses your industry, domain, and goal to decide.
For example:

Industry	Best Platform	Quest Example
Design	Instagram	Post a carousel of UI tips.
Marketing	LinkedIn	Share a client case study.
Tech / Startups	X	Share a mini-thread on lessons learned.
📅 Step 5: Smart Timing & Hashtags

The system already had ML-based posting time recommendations — now it also:

Suggests the best time to post for your industry + region.

Adds AI-generated hashtags based on your domain and audience.

Example:

Best time: 6–8 PM IST
Suggested hashtags: #GrowthMarketing #EcommerceIndia #PersonalBranding

🔁 Step 6: Assign, Track, and Expire

You’ll see your quests every day:

1 Career Quest + 1 Social Quest

Each has clear title, steps, and XP reward.

You can complete, dismiss, or let it expire in 24 hours.

When you complete it:

You earn XP (linked to your goal category, e.g. +40 XP for Visibility).

Over time, the system learns what you engage with more and adjusts future quests accordingly.
