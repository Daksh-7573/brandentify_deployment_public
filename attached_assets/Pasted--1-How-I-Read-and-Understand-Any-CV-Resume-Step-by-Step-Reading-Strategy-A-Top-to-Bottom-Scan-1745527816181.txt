✅ 1. How I Read and Understand Any CV/Resume
🔍 Step-by-Step Reading Strategy:
A. Top-to-Bottom Scan (Initial Impression)

Header: Name, role/title, contact info, LinkedIn/GitHub/Portfolio

Professional Summary (if available): Quick check for clarity, alignment with target roles

Design Check: Is it readable? Not overly cluttered? Font sizes and color contrasts okay?

B. Section-by-Section Analysis

Experience: Most weight is given here

Company name, role, dates

Responsibilities vs Achievements

Impact: Are there metrics, outcomes, or improvements?

Skills: Are they relevant to the target role/industry?

Projects (if included): Especially important for freshers or career switchers

Scope, Tools Used, Outcomes

Education: Does it support the career path? Any honors/certifications?

Certifications/Extras: Languages, volunteering, extracurriculars

🎨 2. How I Handle Complex/Graphical CVs (like Canva ones)
Modern CVs often look beautiful, but as a career expert, here’s how I interpret them:

Ignore design noise: Focus on the substance over style.

Look past icons and visuals: Some icons don’t communicate well without labels.

Check logical flow: Even with creative layouts, does it still follow a clear path (e.g., left to right or top to bottom)?

PDF version matters: I review exported versions—not just how they appear in the builder.

I ensure:

ATS compatibility if needed (some Canva designs fail ATS)

Consistency in font size and headings

White space is balanced (not cramped or too empty)

🧠 3. What I Analyze Deeply

Element	What I Look For
Summary/Objective	Is it personalized and value-focused, not generic?
Work Experience	Quantifiable achievements, relevant tools, leadership or impact stories
Skills Section	Do listed skills match job descriptions? Are soft and hard skills balanced?
Language	Is it active, powerful, and error-free?
Layout & Flow	Is it easy to scan? Does it highlight key value early?
Tailoring	Is it generic or tailored for a specific role/industry?
Red Flags	Gaps, frequent job switches, too much jargon, or vagueness
Career Progression	Is there growth, consistency, and a clear story?
