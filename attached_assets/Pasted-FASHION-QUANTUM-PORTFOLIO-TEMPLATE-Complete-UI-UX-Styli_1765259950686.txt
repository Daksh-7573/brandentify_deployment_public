✒ TYPOGRAPHY (EDITORIAL FEEL)

Fonts (suggested):

Display / Headline: Playfair Display or Cormorant Garamond

Body / UI: Inter / Sora / system sans-serif

Hierarchy:

H1 (Name): Serif, large (28–36px), high contrast, tracking-tight

H2 (Roles/Sections): Serif or Sans, 18–22px, uppercase or small caps

Body: Sans, 14–16px, soft line-height (1.6)

Labels/Chips: All caps, tracking-widest, 10–12px

🧩 CORE UI/UX ELEMENTS
1️⃣ HERO HEADER SECTION (FASHION PROFILE HERO)

Purpose:
Instantly say “I’m a fashion professional, not just a generic user.”

Elements:

Profile Image (Runway Portrait)

Large, vertical-oriented or 1:1

Framed with:

Soft spotlight oval behind image (Champagne Glow → transparent)

Thin border in Soft Bone / Nude

Very subtle glass reflection overlay (top corner gradient)

3-Layer Look (Fashion Version):

Outer Glow: Champagne soft gradient, very low blur (not neon)

Middle Ring: Thin ring in Warm Sand

Inner Border: 1px Soft Bone/white line

No harsh cyan/purple glows. This is soft, editorial light.

Name & Title

Name: Serif, large, white, slightly above center

Underline or hairline rule in Warm Sand

Title: e.g. “Fashion Model · Editorial · Runway” in small caps

Fashion Tags (Chips)

Chips for:

Location (MapPin)

Category (e.g., “Runway”, “Editorial”, “Commercial”)

Agencies or “Independent”

Style:

Thin bordered pill (Soft Bone)

Background: rgba(245,243,238,0.06)

Text: Caps, mini tracking

CTAs (3 Buttons)

“View Portfolio” (primary)

“Book Me” (secondary)

“Download Comp Card” or “Download Resume”

Button Styling:

Primary: Blush Pink → Nude gradient, white text, soft glow

Secondary: Outline style (Soft Bone border, transparent background)

Hover:

Slight lift: translateY(-2px)

Box-shadow: champagne glow

Slight brightening

2️⃣ ABOUT / SNAPSHOT SECTION (FASHION STORY)

Uses: AboutMe, Vision, Mission, UVP, Core Values, Audiences, Industry, Domain, LookingFor, Email

Layout:

Two-column magazine grid:

Left: Story & Identity

Right: Brand & Positioning

Left Column:

“About” block:

Title: “About” or “My Story”

Serif heading, hairline underline

Body copy in light sans-serif

Vision & Mission:

Two small cards stacked horizontally or vertically

Left border or top accent in Nude/Blush

Short, 1–2 line statements

Right Column:

“Who I Am On Set”:

Use UVP text, in slightly larger serif, italic possible

Core Values:

Small tags: “Professional”, “Creative”, “Reliable”, etc.

Warm Sand/Blush tags, rounded

Primary Audience:

“Ideal for” – e.g. fashion brands, magazine editors, agencies

LookingFor:

Row with Target icon and text (e.g., “Looking for: Editorial & Runway work this season”)

Email:

Clean inline link with underlined style

Visual Tone:
Like a bio page in a magazine: lots of negative space, minimal but strong.

3️⃣ SKILLS / SPECIALTIES SECTION (FASHION SKILLSET)

This is less “coding skills” and more:

For models:

“Runway Walk”, “Editorial Posing”, “Commercial Smile”, “High Fashion Concepts”

For photographers:

“Editorial Photography”, “Lookbook Shoots”, “Retouching”, “Studio Lighting”

For stylists:

“Editorial Styling”, “Campaign Styling”, etc.

Card Style:

Tall, narrow cards

Heading: skill name

Subtext: category (e.g., “Runway”, “On-Camera”, “Studio”)

Rating: use subtle dot or bar system, but NO gamified meters

Visual:

Very subtle gradient top border (Blush/Nude)

Hover:

Very light lift

Accent border thickens from 1px → 2px

4️⃣ EXPERIENCE SECTION (BRAND & CLIENTS)

Timeline but editorial style:

Vertical line offset to left

Year labels (small caps) on timeline

Cards for:

Role: “Lead Model – Lakmé Fashion Week”

Brand: e.g., “For: XYZ Designer”

Type: Runway / Editorial / Campaign

Location

Description: 1–2 lines max

Key: visible brands/logos (if allowed visually later)

Styling:

Cards with:

Slight Champagne/Nude top border or corner accent

Serif title, sans body

Current / recent highlight:

Thin highlight bar on card edge

Slight glow or background emphasis

5️⃣ EDUCATION SECTION (TRAINING & WORKSHOPS)

For fashion industry, this becomes:

Modeling schools

Acting workshops

Photography courses

Styling diplomas

Layout:

Simple two-column list or mini-timeline

Each row:

Left: Institution, program

Right: Year(s)

Under: Key skills (chips)

Style:

Understated. Think “CV area” hidden behind aesthetic.

6️⃣ PROJECTS / PORTFOLIO (LOOKBOOK)

This is the key for fashion.

Masonry or symmetrical grid

Card Content:

On hover:

Subtle dark overlay

Show:

Project title (e.g., “Editorial – Winter Noir”)

Client (e.g., “Vogue India Test Shoot”)

Role (Model / Photographer / Stylist / MUA)

Small Eye icon “View Story”

Modal / Detail View:

Fullscreen overlay:

Big hero image

Carousel for extra shots

Text:

Title

Role

Brand/Client

Location

Year

Short story / concept text

Style:

Black + Bone palette

Thin lines

Minimal chrome

7️⃣ SERVICES SECTION (IF PREMIUM – BOOKING / RATE CARD)

For:

Photographers offering shoots

Stylists offering services

Coaches or mentors

Card Layout:

2–3 cards:

Top: Service title

Sub: Category (e.g., “1:1 Portfolio Building Session”)

Pricing:

🎨 STYLING TECHNIQUES (FASHION VERSION)
Glassmorphism

Very subtle:

backdrop-filter: blur(10–16px)

background: rgba(5,5,9,0.72)

border: 1px solid rgba(245,243,238,0.18)

Gradients

Backgrounds:

linear-gradient(135deg, #050509, #111118)

Accents:

linear-gradient(135deg, #F9C5D5, #FDF3D9)

Underlines / small bars:

linear-gradient(90deg, #D9B99B, #FDF3D9)

Borders

Use Thin, elegant lines

Colors:

rgba(245,243,238,0.35) for edges

#D9B99B / #F9C5D5 for accent edges

Clip Paths & Shapes

Rare; prefer:

Soft rectangular cards

Occasional angled accent bar or diagonal soft corner

✨ ANIMATIONS (FASHION-FRIENDLY)

Keep it slow, graceful, editorial.

Hover lifts: translateY(-2px)

Opacity fades: 200–300ms

Shimmer on name or title: very gentle

No bouncy or elastic animations

Optional:

Slow zoom on hero background image

Slow parallax on background shapes

🔲 BACKGROUND & DECORATIVE ELEMENTS

Background:

Deep black → charcoal gradient

Very subtle noise texture (like film grain)

Decorations:

Thin vertical lines (columns) to mimic magazine margins

Occasional soft gradient circles (light blobs)

No heavy grids / circuits. We’re fashion editorial, not tech.

📱 RESPONSIVE DESIGN

Mobile:

Single column

Small margins

Image-first: hero picture should dominate

Tablet:

2 columns where possible (About, Skills, Projects)

Desktop:

Full experience, lots of breathing room

🔍 ICON USAGE (FASHION-APPROPRIATE)

Use lucide-react, but:

MapPin → location of base city

Camera → for photographers

Scissors / Shirt (if custom) → for stylists

Star → featured work

Eye → view project

Calendar → show seasons/years

Heart/Star → “featured” or “favorite”
