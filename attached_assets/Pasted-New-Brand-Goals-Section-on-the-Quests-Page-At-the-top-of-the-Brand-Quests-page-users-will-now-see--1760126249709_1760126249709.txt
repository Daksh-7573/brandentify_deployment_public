New Brand Goals Section on the Quests Page

At the top of the Brand Quests page, users will now see a new section titled:

“🎯 Choose Your Brand Goals”

It explains:

“Select up to 3 goals that match what you want to achieve right now. Your daily Brand Quests will be personalized based on these goals.”

So, before doing any daily quests, users quickly pick the 3 things they care most about.

2️⃣ Four Main Goal Categories

We’ve organized all 12 goals into 4 easy groups:

Visibility & Awareness – (Grow audience, get discovered)

Professional & Career Growth – (Build authority, attract opportunities)

Engagement & Community – (Build and connect with loyal followers)

Monetization & Impact – (Earn, collaborate, and launch products)

Each category expands to show specific goals, like:

“Improve visibility on social media”

“Attract new business opportunities”

“Build a loyal community”

“Launch my own product or service”

3️⃣ Users Can Select Up to 3 Goals

The system lets you pick up to 3 — so it’s focused and not overwhelming.

Once 3 are selected, the others get temporarily disabled (you can deselect to change).

It shows a small counter like “2/3 selected” at the top.

Example:

If I choose:
✅ Increase brand recognition
✅ Attract new business opportunities
✅ Build a loyal community
Then the rest of the goals are greyed out.

4️⃣ Save Goals Button

After choosing, users click “Save Goals” — it saves their preferences to their account.

This triggers a small success message:

“✅ Goals saved! Your daily quests will now adapt to match your focus.”

These saved goals are remembered for each user, so next time they log in, they’ll see their selected goals already active.

5️⃣ Design & UX Experience

We’re making this feel light and easy to use:

Each goal appears as a card or toggle (tap to select, tap again to deselect).

The cards are grouped in collapsible accordion sections (to reduce clutter).

Clean layout with emojis, icons, and colors (consistent with Brandentifier style).

Works great on both mobile (as a bottom slide-up sheet) and desktop (inline panel).

Clear button states:

Disabled until you choose something

“Save” turns blue when goals are picked
