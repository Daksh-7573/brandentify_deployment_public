### 🧠 Fine-Tuned Intelligence on Career Knowledge

Train Musk on a specialized corpus of structured and unstructured career data to build expertise far beyond generic LLMs.

#### 1. **Structured Data for Fine-Tuning**
| Source | Content |
|--------|---------|
| O*NET Database | Skill-role-industry mappings, career ladders |
| Levels.fyi | Compensation data across companies and titles |
| Job Boards (via API/Scraping) | Live job descriptions by role, industry, country |
| LinkedIn Dataset (aggregated metadata) | Career switches, role evolution, skills in demand |
| Resume Libraries | Real-life resume examples by industry and experience |

#### 2. **Unstructured Data for Language Nuance**
| Source | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Career blogs, coaching transcripts | Real coaching dialogue for tone training |
| YouTube interviews/podcasts | Career journeys, emotional inflection cues |
| Reddit & Quora threads | Questions from confused, curious, and motivated users |

#### 3. **Model Outcomes After Fine-Tuning**
- Understand difference between same roles in different industries
- Prioritize relevant skills based on goals, not just listings
- Detect when a resume sounds outdated or generic
- Learn real job-speak vs. corporate jargon

#### 4. **Training Principles**
- Use **contrastive pairs** (bad vs. great resumes, vague vs. strong objectives)
- Embed **role+goal context** in every training instance
- Train with **cross-domain career switches** (e.g., Designer → PM → Founder)
- Apply **weighted loss functions** to reinforce high-value patterns (e.g. mentorship over generic growth tips)
